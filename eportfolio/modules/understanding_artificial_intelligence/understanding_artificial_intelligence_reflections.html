Reflections

Personal reflections on the Induction Module, structured using Gibbs’ Reflective Cycle (What? → Feelings → Evaluation → So What? → What Next?).

Reflection 1: Joining the Online Community

What? (Description)

I introduced myself in the discussion forum, shared expectations, and engaged with peers to compare approaches to online study.

Feelings

At first, I felt hesitant about posting publicly, but encouragement from peers boosted my confidence.

Evaluation

Peer replies validated my strategies and also highlighted areas where I could improve, particularly time management.

So What? (Interpretation)

This interaction demonstrated the importance of learning communities in providing social accountability and motivation. It connected well with Vygotsky’s concept of learning through social interaction.

What Next? (Outcome)

I plan to schedule two short weekly sessions for forum engagement and private reflection to establish active participation as a habit.

Reflection 2: First Group Collaboration

What? (Description)

Worked in a small group to review academic articles and present key findings to peers.

Feelings

I was initially uncertain about group dynamics but later felt motivated by the diversity of perspectives.

Evaluation

The collaboration was productive, though scheduling was challenging. Constructive feedback helped refine our presentation.

So What? (Interpretation)

This activity highlighted the significance of communication tools and leadership in distributed teams. It also illustrated how knowledge sharing accelerates problem-solving.

What Next? (Outcome)

I will improve time management by setting early deadlines for group tasks and using collaboration platforms more effectively.
