Part 2 – Personal Development Action Plan

Where do I want to be by the end of this period? By the end of the next study period, I want to close the specific developmental gaps identified during the Machine Learning module — deepening my ability to justify modelling choices in writing, strengthening my confidence in managing team-dynamic challenges, and extending my technical range into areas the module introduced but did not fully exercise.

What do I want / need to learn? What do I have to do to achieve this? Resources or support needed How will I measure success? Target dates
1. Deepen the written justification of modelling choices
The Unit 6 tutor feedback identified the discussion of algorithm selection as a developmental area. I want to develop a consistent personal standard for documenting why a specific algorithm, hyperparameter or preprocessing decision was chosen, with tabular diagnostics where appropriate.		 Review the Unit 6 tutor feedback and apply the same algorithm-justification discipline to every future technical deliverable. Study worked examples in Bishop (2006) and James et al. (2021) that present model comparisons in tabular form. Module reading lists, e-library access, tutor feedback from subsequent modules. Tutor feedback on the next project no longer flags justification of algorithm choice as a developmental point; I can articulate the trade-offs of each modelling decision in writing without prompting. End of next module
2. Build confidence in navigating team-dynamics challenges earlier
During the Unit 6 project I absorbed coordination difficulties before escalating them to the tutor. I want to develop the professional judgement to raise concerns earlier and propose structured resolutions, rather than compensating through additional coordination effort.		 Read Tuckman (1965) and Belbin (1981) more deeply and apply their frameworks at the start of the next team project. Draft a revised team contract template that includes a conflict-escalation protocol as a standard clause. UoEO study skills resources on teamwork; peer conversations with programme cohort; tutor/mentor input where appropriate. Next team project team contract includes a conflict-escalation clause that is actively used; peer evaluation feedback reflects improved facilitation of open discussion. Next team-based module
3. Extend technical range: SQL and R
The Professional Skills Matrix indicates I am currently only Aware of SQL and R. Both are standard tools in data-science workflows and are not covered in depth in this module's e-portfolio activities.		 Complete a structured introductory course in each (e.g. DataCamp / Coursera / university-provided resources). Apply each to a small personal project that mirrors the Unit 6 Airbnb-style analytical pipeline. Online learning platforms (DataCamp, Coursera, freeCodeCamp); the UoEO e-library; my existing Python project code as a reference for equivalent analyses. Able to complete a small end-to-end analysis in each language independently and move my self-assessment from Aware to Trained. Within 6 months
4. Develop critical evaluation of model evaluation metrics under real conditions
The Unit 10 Model Performance Measurement activity and the CD2 peer discussions highlighted the limitations of single metrics such as ROC-AUC under class imbalance. I want to build deeper fluency in selecting and defending evaluation metrics for a given problem, rather than defaulting to accuracy.		 Replicate Unit 10 experiments on imbalanced datasets; study Saito and Rehmsmeier (2015) and Imani et al. (2026) in depth; practise documenting evaluation-metric choice in every analytical project. arXiv, PLOS ONE papers; scikit-learn documentation; public imbalanced datasets (Kaggle). Able to defend metric choice in writing for any future project and propose multiple complementary metrics where appropriate. Within 3 months
5. Formalise familiarity with professional codes of conduct
The Skills Matrix identifies this as currently Aware only. As a developing ML professional, I want to move beyond abstract ethical awareness to a grounded understanding of specific professional codes relevant to my career path.		 Read the BCS Code of Conduct and the ACM Code of Ethics in full. Map specific clauses to the ethical themes I engaged with in CD1 and CD2, and to the Unit 9 CNN activity on bias and transparency. BCS and ACM websites; Unit 9 and CD2 portfolio entries as a starting point for comparison. Able to cite specific clauses of the BCS or ACM code when discussing ethical dimensions of ML systems in future module work. Within 2 months
