Reflection on this Artefact

Writing the summary post required me to move beyond restating my initial argument and instead synthesise it with the three peer responses. Kapinga grounded the discussion in safety theory (Perrow, 1999; Hollnagel, 2017), Monique introduced cross-sector parallels, and Naeema reframed resilience as a model-level property. Engaging with these perspectives challenged my original framing of the Toyota incident and led me to recognise it as part of a broader pattern, where performance optimisation can undermine resilience when safeguards and human oversight are limited. This also deepened my understanding of dataset-related challenges, as it highlighted how model reliability depends on stable data pipelines. Models may perform well during training but fail when data shifts or degrades (Amodei et al., 2016). Synthesising these viewpoints also strengthened my ability to integrate diverse inputs in an asynchronous environment, an essential skill for effective collaboration in distributed development teams (Amershi et al., 2019).
