Case study: Toyota’s Digital Transformation with IT Governance Frameworks

Discussion Forum

Reflection on this Artefact

This post shows a strong understanding of how IT governance frameworks support organisational strategy, using Toyota as a practical example. A key strength is the clear explanation of how ITIL 4 and COBIT 2019 complement each other, linking service delivery with governance, risk, and compliance. The discussion of digital transformation is also effective, particularly in highlighting the shift toward data-driven decision-making and operational efficiency.
However, the post is largely descriptive and could benefit from deeper critical evaluation. For instance, while ITIL and COBIT are presented positively, their limitations—such as implementation complexity, cost, or organisational resistance—are only briefly touched upon. A more critical comparison with alternative frameworks or approaches would strengthen the analysis.
The inclusion of challenges, such as resistance to change and legacy system integration, adds valuable depth. However, these points could be further developed by assessing how successful the mitigation strategies were, rather than simply describing them.
From a learning perspective, this reflection reinforced the importance of aligning IT strategy with broader business and sustainability goals. In future work, I will focus more on critical analysis, including evaluating trade-offs and the real impact of strategic IT decisions.
